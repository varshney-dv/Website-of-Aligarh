Aligarh

Hamara Aligarh

Aligarh is famous for its lock industry, earning it the nickname "Tala Nagari" or "City of Locks." The city's craftsmen have been producing high-quality locks for centuries, and this industry remains a vital part of Aligarh's economy. In addition to locks, Aligarh's markets are known for brassware, sculpture, and other handicrafts.

AMU

Aligarh Muslim University (AMU), established in 1875 by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan, is one of India's premier educational institutions. Originally founded as the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College, it was designed to provide a modern education to the Muslim population while preserving traditional Islamic values. The institution was elevated to university status in 1920, becoming AMU.

Jawahar Park

Jawahar Park, located in Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh, is a well-known recreational spot that offers a green oasis amidst the hustle and bustle of the city. Named after India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, the park serves as a popular destination for families, students, and fitness enthusiasts alike. The park boasts well-maintained gardens, walking paths, and a variety of trees and plants, providing a tranquil environment for visitors to relax and enjoy nature. It's a favored spot for morning walks, jogging, and picnics. The open spaces and playgrounds within the park make it an attractive place for children to play and engage in outdoor activities..

Aligarh Ki Kachori

Aligarh is renowned for its delicious kachoris, a popular street food that draws food enthusiasts from near and far. Aligarh's kachori is distinctively flavorful, often filled with a spicy mixture of lentils, potatoes, and aromatic spices. The dough, typically made from refined flour, is carefully rolled and stuffed with the filling before being deep-fried to a golden, crispy perfection.
